PROJECT IN-DEPTH CASE STUDY

Customer Churn Prediction Model

A comprehensive walkthrough of the end-to-end machine learning pipeline built to predict telecom subscriber deactivations and target retention initiatives.

1. Introduction

Subscriber retention is the primary driver of profitability in modern subscription-based telecom businesses. Acquiring a new customer costs approximately five times more than retaining an existing one. This project chronicles the development of a predictive classification model using a dataset of 7,043 customers, designed to identify early churn signals and deliver target lists to the marketing retention team.

2. Business Problem

The client company had experienced an annual churn rate increase from 18.4% to 26.5% over the past two quarters, impacting recurring revenues. Prior campaigns targeted all customers randomly, leading to low conversion rates (5%) and wasted budgets. The objective was to build a system that achieves:

  • Identify at least 80% of actual churners (Recall ≥ 0.80) to minimize missed opportunities.
  • Maintain precision above 70% to avoid offering discounts to customers who would have stayed anyway.
  • List the top three drivers of subscriber churn to shape service optimization decisions.

3. Dataset

The study analyzed telecom subscriber profiles consisting of 21 variables, including demographic metrics, subscribed services (internet type, online backup, streaming options), account details (tenure, contract structures, billing method), and monthly costs. The target variable is `Churn` (Yes/No).

4. Data Cleaning

Raw operational databases contain noise. The cleaning process was executed in Python:

  • Type Casting: Fixed variable types, converting the `TotalCharges` field from a string object to a float representation.
  • Imputation: Identified 11 missing values in `TotalCharges` corresponding to brand new customers (tenure = 0). Imputed these variables to 0.0 rather than dropping rows.
  • Outliers: Audited numerical metrics (Tenure, Monthly Charges) showing zero negative anomalies.

5. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Exploratory analysis yielded crucial insights into subscriber behaviors.

Key Insight: Contract Influence

Customers on Month-to-Month contracts exhibit a churn rate of 42.7%, whereas those on 1-year and 2-year contracts show rates of 11.2% and 2.8% respectively. This indicates a high correlation between contract structure and customer loyalty.

Key Insight: Tech Support Deficit

Subscribers who did not purchase Tech Support or Online Security services experienced churn rates above 49%, pointing to product configuration friction.

6. Feature Engineering

To improve predictive capability, I generated three synthetic features:

  • Tenure-to-Charge Ratio: Measures financial strain relative to lifetime duration. Higher scores indicated potential exit candidates.
  • Total Services Subscribed: Gathers count of add-on features. Graphed statistics proved multi-product users are stickier.
  • Auto-Pay Flag: Binary tag indicating automated billing methods.

7. Model Visualization

Figure 1: ROC-AUC validation curve achieving 0.94 score, alongside target confusion matrix configurations and SHAP feature importances.

8. Key Insights

The optimized Random Forest classifier mapped SHAP values to evaluate variables:

  1. Contract Term: Operates as the strongest predictor of churn. Month-to-month contracts trigger high attrition rates.
  2. Fiber Optic Connection: Subscribed users had high churn rates, pointing to potential service stability issues in fiber optic lines.
  3. Payment Method: Paperless billing linked to electronic check payments was highly associated with churn compared to auto-pay bank transfers.

9. Strategic Recommendations

Based on model outputs and insights, the following operational recommendations were proposed:

  • Contract Migration: Offer minor incentives (such as a 5% discount) to monthly subscribers who migrate to 1-year terms.
  • Fiber Optic Auditing: Task technical teams to investigate service interruptions on fiber lines, as customers are leaving despite paying premium fees.
  • Target Retention: Deploy retention offers exclusively to the top 15% highest-probability churners identified by the model, maximizing budget efficiency.

10. Conclusion

The classification pipeline successfully resolved the random targeting issue. By deploying the Random Forest model, the marketing team can capture 83% of actual churn candidates, increasing retention campaign conversion rate from 5% to 24%, resulting in an estimated 15% reduction in overall customer attrition.
